Since the minimum over a set is no greater than the minimum over a subset,

\begin{align}
\gcd(\gcd(a,b),c)
&amp;={\min_{u,v}}^+\!\left({\min_{x,y}}^+(ax+by)u+cv\right)\\
&amp;\ge{\min_{x,y,z}}^+(ax+by+cz)\\
&amp;=\gcd(a,b,c)\tag1
\end{align}

 and so . Thus, by the maximality of the gcd,

\gcd(\gcd(a,b),c)\le\gcd(a,b,c)\tag2

 and  imply that

\gcd(\gcd(a,b),c)=\gcd(a,b,c)\tag3
