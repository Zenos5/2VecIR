Let 
\begin{align}
I’(a)&amp;= \int_0^1\frac{4(1-x)\sinh2a }{1+4 x(1-x) \sinh^2a}dx\\
&amp;= \left[ \frac{\ln[1+4 x(1-x) \sinh^2a]}{\tanh a}
+2\tanh^{-1}\frac{2x-1}{\coth a}\right]_0^1=4a
\end{align}
Then
\int_0^1\frac{\ln(1+x-x^2)}xdx
=\int_0^{\sinh^{-1}\frac12}I’(a)da
= \int_0^{\ln\phi}4a\ da=2\ln^2\phi
